# Mesu Pronunciation Guide

**IPA Reference for the 13 Phonemes of Mesu**

## Overview

Mesu uses a minimal phoneme inventory designed for universal learnability. Every sound exists in the majority of world languages, making Mesu accessible regardless of your native tongue.

| Category | Count | Description |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Consonants | 8 | Stops, nasals, fricative, approximants |
| Vowels | 5 | Standard 5-vowel system |
| **Total** | **13** | Minimal yet complete |

### Notes on Consonants

- **Stops are unaspirated**: /p t k/ are pronounced without the puff of air that English adds in word-initial position. Think of the /p/ in "s**p**in" rather than "**p**in".
- **No voiced stops**: Mesu has no /b d g/. This simplifies the inventory and increases cross-linguistic accessibility.
- **No fricatives except /s/**: No /f v Î¸ Ã° Êƒ Ê’ x h/. If you're tempted to voice the /s/, don't â€” keep it crisp.

### Notes on Vowels

- **Pure monophthongs**: Mesu vowels do not glide. English speakers must resist turning /e/ into [eÉª] or /o/ into [oÊŠ]. Hold the vowel steady.
- **Cardinal vowels**: These are close to the IPA cardinal vowels. If you speak Spanish, Italian, Japanese, or Swahili, your native vowels are nearly perfect for Mesu.
- **No reduced vowels**: Every vowel is fully pronounced. There is no schwa /É™/ and no vowel reduction in unstressed syllables.

## Syllable Structure

Mesu syllables follow the pattern: **CV(n)**

| Pattern | Structure | Examples |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **CV** | Consonant + Vowel | /mi/, /tu/, /ka/, /wo/ |
| **CVn** | Consonant + Vowel + Nasal | /pan/, /lin/, /kon/, /wen/ |

### Constraints

- Every syllable **must** begin with a consonant
- Every syllable **must** have exactly one vowel
- The **only** permitted coda is /n/
- No consonant clusters (no /st/, /pr/, /ks/, etc.)
- No diphthongs (no /ai/, /ou/, etc.)

## Grammar Particles

Grammar particles are **bare vowels** â€” the only elements in Mesu without an initial consonant. This makes them phonotactically unique and instantly recognizable.

| Particle | IPA | Function | Usage |
|:--------:|:---:|----------|-------|
| **i** | /i/ | Verb marker | Precedes action verbs |
| **a** | /a/ | Object/copula marker | Marks objects or "is-noun" |
| **e** | /e/ | Quality marker | Marks "is-adjective" |
| **o** | /o/ | Question marker | Sentence-final question |
| **u** | /u/ | Command marker | Imperative mood |

**Critical rule**: A bare vowel is *always* a grammar particle, *never* part of a content word. This creates an unambiguous parsing firewall.

## Phonotactic Rules Summary

| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **No initial vowels** | Content words always start with a consonant |
| **No clusters** | Maximum one consonant per position |
| **No coda except /n/** | Syllables end in vowel or /n/ only |
| **No diphthongs** | One vowel per syllable, no glides |
| **Bare vowels = grammar** | /i a e o u/ alone are always particles |

## Tips by Native Language

### English Speakers

- Don't aspirate /p t k/ â€” soften them
- Don't diphthongize /e o/ â€” keep them pure
- /a/ is "ah" as in "father", not "a" as in "cat"
- Syllables are evenly timed, not stress-timed

### Spanish/Italian/Japanese Speakers

Your vowels are already correct. Minor adjustments:
- Ensure /w/ is a true approximant, not [gw] or [Î²]
- /s/ is always voiceless, never [z]

### Mandarin Speakers

- No tones in Mesu â€” pitch is not phonemic
- /l/ and /n/ are distinct phonemes (not allophones)
- Unaspirated stops are correct as-is

### French Speakers

- /u/ is not /y/ â€” back rounded, not front rounded
- No nasal vowels â€” /an/ is [an], not [É‘Ìƒ]
- Final /n/ is fully pronounced, not nasalization
