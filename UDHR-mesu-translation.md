# Pan-Lum Ma-Ki-To Wo-Me-To
## Universal Declaration of Human Rights in Mesu

---

## Vocabulary Key

Before the translation, here are the key compound words developed for this text:

| Mesu | Components | Meaning |
|------|------------|---------|
| **ma-ki-to** | good + have + thing | right (entitlement) |
| **wo-me-to** | big + say + thing | declaration |
| **pan-lum** | all + person | humanity, all humans |
| **nu-lin** | new + life | birth, to be born |
| **ma-mo** | good + move | freedom, liberty |
| **san-wi-to** | same + feel + thing | dignity, worth |
| **lu-sop** | know + head | reason, rationality |
| **ma-lu-sop** | good + know + head | conscience |
| **san-lin-wi** | same + life + feel | brotherhood, kinship |
| **ke-me-to** | do + say + thing | law, rule |
| **nam-wi-ke** | bad + feel + do | torture |
| **ki-lum** | have + person | slavery (owning person) |
| **no-ki** | not + have | freedom (not owned) |
| **nam-ke** | bad + do | crime, wrong action |
| **lu-pan-lum** | know + all + person | recognize (as person) |
| **ti-to** | near + thing | protection |
| **wo-lum-to** | big + person + thing | nation, country |

## Article 1 / Me-To Wen

### English:
*All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in a spirit of brotherhood.*

### Mesu:
```
pan-lum i nu-lin a ma-mo kan san.
si-pan i ki a san-wi-to kan ma-ki-to.
si-pan i ki a lu-sop kan ma-lu-sop.
si-pan u ke a san-lin-wi la si-si.
```

### Interlinear:
```
pan-lum    i      nu-lin    a        ma-mo      kan  san
all-person [VERB] new-life  [OBJ]    good-move  and  same
"All humans are born free and equal."

si-pan    i      ki     a        san-wi-to       kan  ma-ki-to
they-all  [VERB] have   [OBJ]    same-feel-thing and  good-have-thing
"They have dignity and rights."

si-pan    i      ki     a        lu-sop      kan  ma-lu-sop
they-all  [VERB] have   [OBJ]    know-head   and  good-know-head
"They have reason and conscience."

si-pan    u      ke     a        san-lin-wi     la       si-si
they-all  [CMD]  do     [OBJ]    same-life-feel toward   each-other
"They should act with brotherhood toward each other."
```

## Article 2 / Me-To Le

### English:
*Everyone is entitled to all the rights and freedoms set forth in this Declaration, without distinction of any kind.*

### Mesu:
```
pan-lum i ki a ma-ki-to pan kan ma-mo pan we ni wo-me-to.
no to i ke a no-san.
```

### Interlinear:

"All people have all rights and all freedoms from this declaration."

no   to     i      ke     a        no-san
not  thing  [VERB] make   [OBJ]    not-same
"Nothing makes [them] different."
```

## Article 4 / Me-To Le-Le

### English:
*No one shall be held in slavery or servitude; slavery and the slave trade shall be prohibited.*

### Mesu:
```

### Interlinear:

## Notes on Translation Choices

1. **ma-ki-to** (good-have-thing) for "right" — captures the sense of entitlement to something good
2. **san-wi-to** (same-feel-thing) for "dignity" — the feeling of equal worth
3. **ma-mo** (good-move) for "freedom/liberty" — the ability to move/act as one wishes
4. **ke-me-to** (do-say-thing) for "law" — rules that are spoken and done
5. **san-lin-wi** (same-life-feel) for "brotherhood" — the feeling of shared life/kinship
6. **nam-wi-ke** (bad-feel-do) for "torture" — causing bad feeling deliberately
7. **ki-lum** (have-person) for "slavery" — owning a person

The translation aims for clarity within Mesu's 48-root constraint while preserving the document's meaning and dignity.
