## Sentence Patterns

```
Subject i Verb a Object    "I drink water" = mi i wan a was
Subject a Noun             "I am a person" = mi a lum
Subject e Quality          "Water is cold" = was e nip
[sentence] o               "Do you move?" = tu i mo o
u Verb (a Object)          "Drink water!" = u wan a was
no + X                     "not X" = no X
```

## Word Order

- **Compounds:** Modifier + Head → `wo-lum` = big-person = giant
- **Possession:** Possessor + Possessed → `mi was` = my water
- **Tense:** `pa` (past) / `ne` (now) / `la` (future) before verb

## Core Roots (48)

**Existence:** ta (be), to (thing), su (stuff)

**Pronouns:** mi (I), tu (you), si (it), ni (this), ka (what)

**Qualities:** wo (big), pi (hot), wa (wet), ko (hard), lo (bright), ma (good), nu (new), sa (fast)

**Spatial:** so (up), na (in), pe (front), ti (near)

**Temporal:** pa (before), ne (now), lon (long)

**Quantity:** wen (1), le (2), mu (many), pan (all)

**Actions:** ke (do), mo (move), ki (have), se (give), ku (take), li (see), te (hear), wi (feel), lu (know), pu (want), me (say), lin (live)

**Logic:** no (not), kan (and), kon (or), sin (if)

**Relations:** we (of), la (to), win (with), san (same), pen (part)
